[Home](index.html) ## Introduction to Web Development: Meet Page [Next Lesson >>](lesson-htmlcss-1.html) Websites are generally composed of the golden trio of HTML, CSS, and Javascript. A good way of remembering what each does is by comparing a web page to a person. - A Person has a frame holding them up loosely (**HTML**) - And each person has their own creative, specific, style (**CSS**) - And finally, people interact with things and respond when spoken to (**Javascript**) The structure of a website isn't so different from that of your average human friend. It has a skeleton, a distinguishable style, and ideally a lot of fun under-the-hood features that make interacting with it a joy and not a chore. Let's meet Page. <img src="images/Page.jpg" alt="Page" style="width: 25%; height: 25%"> As you can see, Page is stylish and well-maintained. She's a great lecturer and presents her arguments in a structured, concise manner. If you want her to be more than a face in the crowd, Page is interactive - you could even potentially chat with her, play a game of chess, or ask her to direct you to some of her friends. Like Page, an actual webpage should have all of the above attributes by implementation through **CSS**, **HTML**, and **JavaScript**, respectively. Let's dive into web development.
