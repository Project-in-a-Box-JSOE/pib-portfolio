I like to think about how the web works as how a mobile food truck works. Inherent in the world-wide web is a flow of data, and several posts that intercept the data. There are protocols and standards, but a really simple way of thinking about it is imagining it as a food truck. There's a storefront that takes all your requests, and makes their own requests, goes to the kitchen in the back to parse your request (order), which the chef and crew decides how to server you your meal (content). In this scenario, you have servers that server content (data).
